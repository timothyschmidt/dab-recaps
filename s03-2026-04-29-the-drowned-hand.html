Events

Imar Marrowhands at the Slaughter Ramp ~11:00–25:00

Picking up where s02 ended, the party was on the Slaughter Ramp / dock area. Vergrad showed off a new piece of his work — he'd taken a chair from the inn and woodcarved it into a quarterstaff with new roots, one leg deliberately too long so the stub legs wobble. He explained his craft as the inverse of woodworking: rather than removing material to find a shape, he draws shape out of wood by remoulding the grain. ~11:34–12:30

The party walked the Slaughter Ramp / Flesh Pillar Bay docks looking for Imar. They were directed toward a Rewritten Dockmaster — balding, spectacled, late 40s, grafted (one elvish ear, one normal), carrying a ledger and flanked by three guards. After a snubbing first attempt by Lucien ("did that mean anything to me, boy?"), Elenor tossed a coin onto his ledger ~19:56, and the Dockmaster pointed them to the Fourth Birthing, telling them they'd hear "some sort of jolly fucking tune."

On approach, Imar was singing a sea shanty:

Sloops in the harbour, souls lost at sea / Way ho, dark seas roam on / The crew in the tavern drowns with me / When the frost lights sing our song / Short on the voyage, minds all gone / Welcome, lost souls, the void sends anew / All the malice known only to few / We sail for terror, risen tyrant sea / When the debt collects, we break the king free / So shoot up your sirach and drink down your ale / Way ho, dark seas roam forever on / She'll steal your marrow, hands to the rail / Way ho to the deathless throne. ~21:30–22:43

Lucien's history check on the song ~25:00–28:05

Brett gave Lucien the following confirmed information from the lyrics:

  • "Frost lights sing our songs" / "minds all gone" — references the Sea of Souls and how its lights lure people; time spent out there steals memory; the dead are "as good as dead" once the void's taken them. Player-known
  • Void Taint is unshakable — leaving any large dwelling settlement for any extended period is a continuous problem. People congregated into towns because that's the only way they found to abate it. Player-known
  • "We sail for terror, risen tyrant sea" — risen tyrant is a historical moniker for Atrum Rex. The song is a shanty paying respect; Imar is well aware whose ocean he sails. Player-known
  • Neko got a separate (disadvantaged) history check; Brett gave him only general "you're a bit removed" flavour.

The journey out and the hand of a drowned god ~40:00–~103:00

Sailing through Flesh Pillar Bay toward the hunting grounds — fluid souls-mixed water (always purple/blue, with hollow shadowy faces visible if you stare into it; freezes on the surface, requiring the sloop's metal-forged ramming point to break thinner ice shelves). Imar mentioned the shark guts hunting grounds are receding outward over time. ~43:20

About 56 minutes in ~55:57, the ice shelves rocked the sloop and a colossal hand emerged from the ice ~300 ft away — clear ice, frosty ice, and rock; a soft pulsing white light emanating in waves. Only emerged to about the wrist; what's below is unknown but "fucking massive."

Initial party reactions

  • Imar reflexively turned the sloop 45° away from the hand.
  • Imar gave Bardic Inspiration (D8) to Yorika, then Neko, Vergrad. (Imar is a bardcaster — Spell Save DC and Cha mod +5.) The inspiration carries two-mode rules: standard add-to-attack/check (with optional Wis-save frighten rider) OR damage-reduction reaction (die + Cha mod). DM ruling
  • Imar fired a flintlock at the hand at extreme range; missed, then failed his own save and got addled. The boat started turning back toward the hand as he charmed-steered it.

The light's effect

  • Round 1: 1 damage on failed save, paralysis-condition / charm-effective. Vergrad failed his Con save first.
  • Round 2+: 8 psychic damage to anyone who could see the light and failed the Con save. Vergrad, Elenor, and Lucien all failed and took 8 each. Yorika was charmed (immune as charmed creature). Ashen was blindfolded and immune.
  • The hand moved ~70 ft per round, breaking through ice toward the boat.

Solutions the party came up with (Brett explicitly praised this)

  • Lucien — at the wheel, broke the strength contest to keep the boat turning away. Then covered his eyes. ~89:30
  • Ashen — pulled the strip from his robe, tied it as a blindfold, took the dodge action — verified immune to the light effect. ~81:36
  • Yorika — copied Ashen, ripped a strip from her cloak as a blindfold.
  • Vergrad — when charmed, druidcrafted into the deck wood to grab the rudder mentally; his charm twisted the ship the wrong way, but he broke the rudder in the process. Boat ended with a slight permanent list right; Imar later quietly fixed it.
  • Neko — assisted at the helm; later cleverly smeared his clear-glass canister with hydraulic fluid from his earlier blood-pact wound so he couldn't see the light through it. Brett loved this. ~95:00
  • Elenor — first in-play use of her batkin form. Per Adam: Elenor mechanically is a Batkin, but normally appears as a beautiful human-passing woman and blends into society; she can switch form to access her racial traits. Used Echolocation (30 ft blindsight, assumed Nightwing subrace) to navigate blindfolded; flew up to a sail and beat her wings to give the sloop an extra half-movement boost. Cast Bleak Flame on herself for theatrical healing as she returned to human-passing form. (Origin feat — Hunter of Hunters / Dead Aim: ignores resistances on hit.)

Eventually the boat moved 250 ft away. The hand sank back beneath the ice, lights winking out finger by finger. ~102:35

Imar's reveal post-encounter — ~104:43 "That, I believe, is the hand of a drowned god. It's not the first I've come across, but I hope it's the last." Player-known
Potential story thread (not fact): Established lore documents at least one "drowned" entity — The Drowned King, listed in death_rules.md as Pact Entity #8 ("once a god of dominion, now sunken and broken beneath the Sea of Souls"). The Fionrir reveal ~228:33

This whole section is L&E only in-character; OOC for the rest of the party.
  • Imar's lyric "we break the king free" + "deathless throne" / "risen tyrant sea" connect Imar to Atrum Rex ("the Unburied King"). Aligns with established lore — Atrum Rex's seat is the Deathless Throne / Sorrowhold Bastion in Jotungore.
  • The Rewritten and Atrum Rex's faction are distinctly two separate factions. The Rewritten are not Gravelord-backed — they're operating in Atrum Rex's own domain without his support.
  • The Undying Accord has existed for ~80 years (written early 3900s YPE). It says no faction may move openly against another. This is why non-Gravelord-supported factions cannot openly attack Gravelord-aligned ones, and vice versa — open conflict would cause political turmoil. This is why Imar can openly recruit on the docks.
  • Brett, above-table: "Eleanor specifically [was] sent here because Fionrir has gained power that is rivaling what they don't want her to have. It is too far beyond their levels, and they're like, that needs to be found out, monitored, and watched."
  • Gravelords are not created — they earn their power over time. Fionrir could be a potential usurper.
Spelling/term notes: Brett spoke "Fjornrir" repeatedly in this scene; canonical name is Fionrir Starsundr. Brett used "Undying Court" once; canonical term is Undying Accord — treat the transcript's "Court" as a verbal misspeak.

DM rulings & rule clarifications

  • House Rule Nat 20 on skill checks = critical success — regardless of bonuses or DC. Formalised this session. ~133:17
  • 2014 → 2024 Underwater attack disadvantage does NOT apply on the surface. Only fully submerged combat triggers the penalty. ~187:42
  • 2024 RAW Swim without swim speed = half movement. No ability check needed for routine swimming.

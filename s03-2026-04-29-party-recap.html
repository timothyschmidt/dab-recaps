Session III

The Hand of a Drowned God

Wednesday 29 April 2026 · Full table
Tim (Neko) · Adam (Elenor) · Michael (Lucien) · Scott (Ashen) · Reign (Vergrad) · Joel (Yorika)
The party signed on with a skeletal sea-captain named Imar Marrowhands ("it's pronounced Ivar, the spelling lies") for a single-day shark-guts hunt out of the Slaughter Ramp. About an hour into the journey, a colossal hand of a drowned god rose out of the ice, started crushing minds with pulsing white light, and yanked the boat off course while half the party was charmed. The party blindfolded their way out of it with cloth strips, hat brims, and Neko's own hydraulic fluid smeared on his face plate. Elenor switched into her batkin form for the first time in play (she normally appears human-passing) and used echolocation to fly the rigging while blind. They lived; the hand sank back beneath the ice. The actual shark-guts hunt that followed was textbook by comparison — gunpowder satchel + Imar's loaned flintlock pistol blew the corpse open, exposed the parasite tentacles, Ashen got grappled into the void-water, and Neko leapt off the rail to break him free. Lucien picked up his first level of Void Taint (whispers + Wis disadvantage); Yorika escalated to level 2 (now also blinded in bright light). Imar paid the full 20 gp, then quietly recruited the party as spies on the Rewritten. Back at the inn, Julian dropped a bombshell: Sultan Keshen knows there are two vampires in town. And above-table, Brett confirmed: Fionrir Starsundr is rising in power — that's why Lilith sent Elenor. Drinks with Imar tomorrow.

What We Now Know

  • At least one "drowned god" exists. Imar has fought a hand of one before this one. Nothing is known about what's beneath the wrist. Possible connection to the established Drowned King Pact entity — speculative, not confirmed.
  • Imar serves Atrum Rex — confirmed via his shanty (the "risen tyrant sea" / "deathless throne" lyrics) and Lucien's history check.
  • Ottahofn is in Jotungore = Atrum Rex's domain. His seat is Sorrowhold Bastion / the Deathless Throne. The party is currently in his territory.
  • Atrum Rex and the Rewritten are different factions. The Rewritten are NOT backed by a Gravelord — they're operating in Atrum Rex's own domain without his support.
  • Gravelords earn their power over time — they aren't appointed. The Undying Accord is ~80 years old; it prevents open faction war, which is why Imar can openly recruit on the docks without consequence.
  • Sultan Keshen knows "two vampires are in town" but hasn't ID'd Lucien & Elenor specifically yet. Ticking clock.
  • The Order of the Ashen Rose (Keshen's faction) ultimately serves Gravelord Saint Isdor in Gravenholme.
  • Shark guts mechanics: parasite inside dead host. Body is just a shield (+2 AC). Parasite has Damage Transfer while grappling. Gunpowder works.
  • Void Taint is unshakable outside settlements. Fur coats give advantage on cold-weather saves. Long rests + good rolls cleanse it.
Above-table for Lucien & Elenor only: Lilith sent Elenor specifically to monitor Fionrir Starsundr, who is gaining power that rivals what Lilith doesn't want her to have. Fionrir may be a potential gravelord-tier usurper.

Brett's Big Speech

Mid-combat with the drowned hand, Brett pulled the party out and asked them to think outside the rules-as-written box: cover your eyes, move the wheel, shake your teammates out of stupors, mess with the rigging. The party listened — Elenor went bat, Ashen blindfolded with his robe strip, Vergrad druidcrafted into the rudder, Neko smeared hydraulic fluid on his face plate. Brett: "I'd encourage a bit more of that. Yeah."

Combat is going to keep being weird and creative for a while; Brett wants level 3 sooner rather than later so the party gets subclass features and stops feeling like one missed attack ends a turn.
